# AI Institute Africa Announces Partnership to Upskill 500 Staff in Responsible AI

### University of Nairobi to Spearhead Comprehensive Responsible AI Curriculum

**Nairobi, Kenya — June 10, 2026** — AI Institute Africa today announced a new partnership with the University of Nairobi to train 500 public-sector employees in responsible artificial‑intelligence practices, reinforcing the continent’s commitment to ethical and inclusive technology deployment.

The initiative will launch in September 2026 and will run across six modules that cover AI ethics, governance, bias mitigation, and end‑to‑end lifecycle management.  University of Nairobi faculty from the Computer Science and Ethics departments will deliver the curriculum, while AI Institute Africa will provide certification and ongoing support.

"We are committed to empowering Africa’s workforce with the skills to build AI responsibly, ensuring technology serves society equitably," said Dr. Jane Doe, CEO of AI Institute Africa.  "This partnership demonstrates how academia and industry can collaborate to accelerate ethical AI adoption across the public sector."

The partnership comes at a time when African governments are expanding digital services and seeking to embed ethical considerations into AI projects.  AI Institute Africa will monitor outcomes through a joint evaluation framework and will begin a second cohort in 2027.

"We look forward to working with AI Institute Africa to equip our staff with critical responsible‑AI knowledge," said Prof. John Kamau, Dean of the School of Computer Science, University of Nairobi.

### About AI Institute Africa
AI Institute Africa, founded in 2023, is a pan‑African organization providing AI education, research, and policy guidance.  The institute partners with governments, academia, and industry to promote ethical AI practices that drive inclusive growth.
