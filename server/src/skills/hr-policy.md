# Skill: HR policy draft

## Goal

An employee reads it once and knows what's expected and what happens if they don't; a manager can apply it consistently; and it holds up if challenged because it's specific, fair, and lawful.

## Structure

```
# <Policy name> Policy
**Effective:** <date> · **Owner:** <HR / role> · **Applies to:** <who> · **Review:** <annually>

## 1. Purpose
<Why this policy exists — the behaviour or risk it addresses.>

## 2. Scope
<Who it covers (all staff / contractors / specific roles), where, and any exclusions.>

## 3. Policy (the rules)
- <Clear, specific statements of what is required / permitted / prohibited.>

## 4. Procedure (how it works in practice)
1. <step — who does what, by when>
2. …

## 5. Roles & responsibilities
| Role | Responsibility |
|---|---|

## 6. Non-compliance
<What happens — linked to the disciplinary process; proportionate and consistent.>

## 7. Related policies & legal basis
<Cross-references; relevant labour law / regulation.>
```

## Rules

- **Plain language, second person.** "You must request leave at least 5 working days ahead" — not "Employees shall endeavour to…".
- **Specific and measurable.** Numbers, timeframes, named approvers — vagueness creates inconsistent enforcement and disputes.
- **Procedure separate from policy.** The rule (what) vs the steps (how) — so you can update the process without re-approving the policy.
- **Consistent + fair.** Consequences proportionate and applied equally; tie to the existing disciplinary framework.
- **Lawful.** Flag where local labour law sets a floor (notice, leave, dismissal) — don't write below it. Note "review with legal/counsel before adoption".
- **Effective date + review cadence + owner** on every policy.

## Pitfalls

- Legalese no one reads — if employees can't understand it, it can't be followed.
- Rules with no procedure — leaves application to each manager's mood.
- Silent on consequences — a policy with no teeth is a suggestion.
- Copy-pasting another country's policy over local labour law.
- No review date — stale policies become unenforceable and unfair.
