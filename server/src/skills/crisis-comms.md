# Skill: Crisis holding statement + Q&A

## Goal

Within minutes of an incident, give spokespeople a short, honest, approved statement that buys time without admitting unproven liability — plus a Q&A so everyone says the same thing.

## Holding statement (the one paragraph everyone uses)

```
<Org> is aware of <factual description of the situation> that began at <time, if known>.
We take this seriously and our priority is <people / safety / customers / data>.
We are <action being taken now> and are working to <restore / investigate / support>.
We will share an update by <specific time>. <Where to get updates: status page / contact>.
```

## Anticipated Q&A

```
| Question (incl. the hostile ones) | Approved answer | Do NOT say |
|---|---|---|
| What happened? | <only confirmed facts> | speculation on cause |
| Was anyone harmed / data lost? | <what's confirmed; "we're verifying X"> | unverified reassurance |
| Whose fault is it? | "Our focus right now is X; a full review will follow." | blame, naming individuals |
| When will it be fixed? | <next-update time, not a promise> | a guessed ETA |
| Is it safe now? | <only if verified> | "yes" before you know |
```

## Rules

- **Acknowledge fast, even with little info.** Silence reads as guilt or incompetence. A holding line at T+15min beats a perfect statement at T+3h.
- **Only confirmed facts.** "We are investigating" is a complete, honest answer. Never speculate on cause or scope.
- **No admission of legal liability** before facts are in — but no defensiveness either. Empathy first, blame never.
- **Commit to a next update TIME, not an outcome.** "Update by 3pm" is keepable; "fixed by 3pm" is a hostage to fortune.
- **One voice.** Route all inbound to the named spokesperson; give everyone else the holding line + "I'll connect you with X".
- Include the **bridge phrases** ("What I can tell you is…", "Our focus right now is…") so spokespeople redirect from traps.

## Pitfalls

- Over-reassuring before verification ("no data was lost") then having to walk it back — destroys trust twice.
- Speculating on cause to seem in control — becomes the headline if wrong.
- No update commitment — the vacuum gets filled by others.
- Legalese-only statement with no empathy — reads as cold and adversarial.
