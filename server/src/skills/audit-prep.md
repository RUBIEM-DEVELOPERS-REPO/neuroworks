# Skill: Audit prep checklist

## Goal

Walk into the audit able to produce evidence for every in-scope control on request, with known gaps already being remediated — no scramble, no surprises.

## Structure

```
# Audit readiness — <framework/standard> · <period> · <auditor>
Scope: <systems / processes / entities in scope> · Audit date: <___>

## Control → evidence map
| # | Control / requirement | Evidence to show | Location / owner | Status |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Access reviewed quarterly | Q1–Q4 access review sign-offs | <link> / IT | ✅ ready |
| 2 | Data retention enforced | retention policy + deletion logs | <link> / DPO | ⚠ partial |
| 3 | … | | | ❌ gap |

## Gaps & remediation
| Gap | Risk | Remediation | Owner | Due (before audit) |
|---|---|---|---|---|

## Document request list (have these ready)
- Policies (current, approved, dated)
- Logs / records covering the audit period
- Prior audit findings + evidence they were closed
- Org chart / roles / access matrix

## Logistics
- Auditor liaison: <name> · Interviewees briefed: <who> · Walkthrough booked: <date>
```

## Rules

- **Map every in-scope control to specific evidence** and where it lives — "we do that" is not evidence; a dated, signed artefact is.
- **Evidence must cover the whole audit period**, not just today.
- **Surface gaps yourself, early.** A known gap with a remediation plan and owner is far better than the auditor finding it.
- **Close prior findings first** — repeat findings are the worst look.
- **Brief the interviewees** — consistent, honest answers; "I don't know, I'll get the owner" is fine, bluffing is not.

## Pitfalls

- Treating prep as document-gathering the night before — evidence has to predate the audit.
- "We have a policy" with no proof it's followed (logs, sign-offs).
- Hiding a gap and hoping — it surfaces as a finding plus a trust hit.
- Evidence that only covers the last week of a 12-month period.
