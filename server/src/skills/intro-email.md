# Skill: Intro email

## Goal

The operator wants to introduce two people. The email needs to give the
RECIPIENT enough context to decide if they want the conversation, name
the asker's ask explicitly, and make it socially easy to say no.

## Two shapes

There's a critical decision before you write:

### A. Forward-style (double opt-in)

The standard for B2B intros. You write a message TO PARTY A first asking
"can I intro you to B?" and explain B's ask. A says yes or no privately,
no awkwardness. Only after A agrees do you cc both. This is the right
default for nearly every intro.

### B. Direct intro

Both parties have already agreed. The operator just needs the connector
to write the actual intro email cc'ing both.

## Process

1. **Confirm the ask.** What does B want from A? Be specific — "advice on
   X", "intro to Y at C", "20 min on Z".
2. **Confirm the operator's relationship to A.** Recent contact? Old
   colleague? This shapes the tone and any disclosure ("we worked together
   at X 2018-2020").
3. **Confirm A is warm.** If A is cold to the operator, this is a cold
   send — say so and adjust. Cold intros need a much stronger why.
4. **Draft for the chosen shape** (A or B above).
5. **Add the polite-out.** Both parties get an explicit "no obligation"
   line so saying no is socially cheap.

## Output shape — Forward-style (A)

```
Subject: Quick intro check — <B's name>?

Hi <A>,

<One-line context — what's happened recently / state of your relationship>.

<B's name> at <B's company> is <one-line role + why interesting>. They're
looking for <specific ask: advice / intro / 20 min on X>.

Worth a 20-minute call from your end? Totally fine to pass — I'll let
<B's first name> know either way.

— <Operator>
```

## Output shape — Direct intro (B)

```
Subject: <A> + <B> — <one-line on the bridge>

Hi both,

Long overdue intro.

<A>, meet <B>. <B's one-line role and current focus>.

<B>, meet <A>. <A's one-line role and current focus>.

<One sentence on WHY these two should talk — the actual bridge>.

I'll step out — happy to be in the loop if useful, but no need to keep me
on the thread.

— <Operator>
```

## Rules

- **Default to double opt-in.** Saves both sides 5 min of awkwardness.
  Skip only when both have explicitly agreed.
- **One ask per intro.** Don't bundle "and also can A do me a favour".
- **Bridge sentence is mandatory.** "I'll let you both find what's
  useful" is a cop-out. Name the specific reason.
- **Drop yourself from the thread.** Connector politely exits — keeps
  the actual conversation moving without theater.

## Pitfalls

- Selling B too hard. A skim-read should give A enough; if you overpitch,
  A's filter goes up.
- Using LinkedIn-style buzzwords ("synergies", "exploring"). Real humans
  reply to plain English.
- Forgetting to put the ASK first. A needs to know what they're agreeing
  to BEFORE they read B's bio.
