# Skill: Job description writing

## Goal

A JD a strong candidate reads and thinks: "I could see myself doing this, and the bar is clear." Not a wall of requirements. Not a buzzword salad.

## Structure

```
# <Role Title> at <Company>

<One-line tagline: who we are + what's distinctive>

## What you'll ship in your first 90 days
- <Concrete deliverable 1>
- <Deliverable 2>
- <Deliverable 3>

## What you'll own beyond 90 days
- <Long-term scope: the systems / accounts / surfaces this role owns>
- <…>

## What we need from you
- <Must-have 1 — specific skill or experience tied to an outcome>
- <Must-have 2>
- <Must-have 3>
(3-5 max. If you can't trim to 5, the role isn't defined.)

## What's nice to have
- <Optional: tools, domain experience that'd accelerate ramp>

## How we work
- <One paragraph: location, hours overlap, async vs sync, tooling philosophy>

## Compensation & equity
<Specific range — never "competitive". Equity range with strike-date framing.>

## How to apply
<The actual step. No "send your resume to careers@" if the link is what we want.>
```

## Rules

- **Outcomes before requirements.** Lead with what they'll DO, then what they need to do it. Reversing this loses candidates who scan the first paragraph.
- **3-5 must-haves max.** Long requirement lists filter out qualified candidates and don't filter out unqualified ones.
- **Name compensation.** Hiding comp is a signal of low trust — and the strongest candidates have offers.
- **Strip "rockstar / ninja / 10x".** They flag the writer, not the candidate.
- **Replace "X years of experience" with the outcome.** "5+ years of Go" → "comfortable shipping production Go services that survive on-call without you".
- **Make the "nice to have" actually optional.** Not a smuggled requirement.

## Pitfalls

- **Buzzword soup.** "Synergistic", "fast-paced", "wear many hats" — they describe the writer's vocabulary, not the role.
- **Generic culture paragraph.** "We value collaboration" is meaningless. "Standups are async, deep work blocks are protected, on-call is one week every 8" is real.
- **Hidden ageism / pedigree filters.** "Recent graduate of a top university", "high-energy" — both filter and signal.
- **No close.** JD that ends with the requirements list and no "how to apply" wastes the funnel.
- **List of nice-to-haves longer than must-haves.** Candidates read it as "actually all required".
