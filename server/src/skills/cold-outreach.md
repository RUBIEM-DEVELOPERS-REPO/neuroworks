# Skill: Cold outreach sequence

## Goal

A prospect who's never heard of you replies — because each message is short, about THEM, makes one relevant point, and asks for one small thing.

## Sequence shape (3-5 touches)

```
## Touch 1 — the opener (Day 0)
Subject: <4-6 words, lowercase, not salesy — a curiosity or relevance hook>
<First line: something specific about THEM (trigger event, role, recent news) — proves it's not a blast.>
<One sentence: the value hypothesis — the problem you suspect they have + the outcome you drive.>
<Soft CTA: "Worth a quick look?" / "Open to a 15-min next week?" — one ask, low commitment.>

## Touch 2 — value-add (Day 3)
<Reply on thread. Share a useful resource / proof point / relevant customer result. No "just bumping this".>

## Touch 3 — different angle (Day 7)
<Reframe the value around a different pain or persona; short.>

## Touch 4 — social proof (Day 12)
<Specific result for a comparable company, with a number.>

## Touch 5 — break-up (Day 18)
<"Looks like the timing's off — I'll close the loop. If <pain> becomes a priority, here's where to find me.">
```

## Rules

- **First line is about them, not you.** A researched, specific opener earns the next sentence.
- **One value hypothesis, one CTA per message.** Multiple asks = no reply.
- **Short.** ≤90 words for touch 1; thumb-readable on a phone.
- **Each follow-up adds something** (resource, angle, proof) — never "just following up".
- **Soft, low-friction CTA** early ("worth a look?"), specific ask later ("Tue or Thu?").
- **The break-up email often gets the reply** — make it gracious, not guilt-trippy.

## Pitfalls

- Leading with your company/product story — they don't care yet.
- Spray-and-pray openers ("Hope you're well!") — instant delete.
- Walls of text and three CTAs.
- "Just bumping this to the top of your inbox" — adds zero value, trains them to ignore you.
- Over-personalising with creepy detail — relevant, not stalkerish.
