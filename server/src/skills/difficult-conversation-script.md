# Skill: Difficult-conversation script

## When to use this

Performance management, layoffs, scope resets, telling a peer they're
wrong on something important, breaking off a vendor relationship. Any
1:1 conversation where the other side WON'T want to hear what's coming.

## Goal

The other person walks away with three things:
1. **Clarity** on what changed and why.
2. **Dignity** intact — no surprises, no theatre, no padding.
3. **The next step** — what happens now, what they should do, who else
   knows.

The operator walks away knowing they handled it like a professional.

## Process

1. **Name the kind of conversation.** PIP? Termination? Demotion? Project
   cancellation? Honest peer-to-peer push-back? Each has a different
   structure — don't conflate.
2. **Write the headline first.** One sentence the other person needs to
   hear before anything else. Say it in the first 30 seconds of the
   conversation, not the last 5 minutes.
3. **State the facts, not the feelings.** "Three of the last four
   sprints missed the date" — concrete and falsifiable. "You don't seem
   committed" — vague and combative.
4. **Pre-write their likely questions.** Why? Why now? Why me? What's
   the next step? What do I tell my team / family? Have answers ready.
5. **Plan the next step BEFORE the conversation.** No "we'll figure it
   out together" — that's not your job in this moment, and it leaves
   them in limbo.

## Output shape

```
# <Conversation type> with <Name> — <YYYY-MM-DD>

## Headline (open with this)
> <One sentence — what's true, what's changing>

## Facts
- <Concrete, dated, specific>
- <…>

## The decision
- <What's changed>
- <Effective when>
- <What's NOT changing>

## Their likely questions
1. **Why now?** — <prepared answer>
2. **What did I do wrong?** — <prepared answer; honest, specific>
3. **What's the next step?** — <prepared answer with timeline>
4. **Who else knows?** — <prepared answer>
5. **What do I tell my team / family?** — <prepared answer>

## What to AVOID saying
- <Soft language that creates ambiguity, e.g. "let's see how it goes">
- <Anything HR/legal hasn't cleared>
- <Comparisons to other people on the team>

## Logistics
- Channel: <in-person if possible; video if remote; never Slack>
- Time: <morning so they have day to absorb; never Friday afternoon>
- Length: <15-20 min — don't drag it out>
- Follow-up: <written confirmation in 24h>
```

## Rules

- **No surprises.** If you're firing someone, they should already know
  things weren't going well. If they're shocked, you failed earlier.
- **No mixed messages.** "Great person, but…" softens the message into
  ambiguity. Say the hard thing clean, then the appreciation.
- **One owner of next steps.** Don't leave the other side wondering
  what's expected from them next.
- **Loop legal/HR BEFORE the conversation** if it's termination,
  comp change, or anything with policy implications.

## Pitfalls

- Padding with sympathy until the message is buried.
- Promising things you can't control ("you'll find something amazing").
- Negotiating in the room when the decision is already made — invites
  bargaining and erodes the decision.
- Doing it on Friday or before a long weekend — gives them no recovery
  time, looks cowardly.
- Letting the conversation drift to debate. State, listen, close.
