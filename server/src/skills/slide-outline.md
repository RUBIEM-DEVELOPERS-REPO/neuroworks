# Skill: Slide outline

## Goal

The presenter has a structure they can drop into Keynote/Slides in 15 minutes — every slide has a single message, a title that IS that message, and a note on what to show.

## Process

1. **Identify the AUDIENCE and the ASK.** Board / customer / team / investor — each demands a different shape. The "ask" is what you want the audience to do or believe afterward.
2. **Outline the ARC** in 7-12 slides (anything longer loses the audience; shorter forces them to fill gaps). The classic shape:
   - Slide 1: Title + ask
   - Slide 2: Why this matters now (the problem / opportunity)
   - Slides 3-5: The argument / data
   - Slides 6-8: What we propose / decided
   - Slide 9-10: Risks + asks
   - Slide 11: Next steps
   - Slide 12: Appendix marker
3. **Each slide title is a SENTENCE that conveys the message** — not "Q3 Pipeline" but "Q3 pipeline grew 23% but enterprise slipped".
4. **Three bullets max per slide.** More and the audience reads instead of listens.
5. **Suggest a visual per slide.** Chart, screenshot, photo, single number, diagram — what makes the message land.

## Output shape

```
# Slide outline: <Title>

**Audience:** <Board / exec team / customer / team / investor>
**Ask:** <One sentence on what you want the audience to do / believe afterward>

## Pre-flight checklist
```

## Rules

- **Title = message.** "Q3 Results" is not a title; "Q3 results: revenue up 12%, churn down 3pts, ahead of plan" is.
- **One message per slide.** If you can't summarise the slide in one sentence, it's two slides.
- **Visual every slide.** A bullet-only slide is a place the audience checks email.
- **Speaker notes are the LINE you'd say** — not a paragraph of context.
- **Always end with a next-steps slide** — slide decks without owners and dates fade out.

## Pitfalls

- Burying the ask on the last slide — open with it and reinforce on the last.
- Reading your slides — speaker notes exist so the slide doesn't have to.
- Using stock chart shapes for low-data points (a "chart" of three numbers is just a table — pick the right form).
- Padding to "professional" 30-slide length — a 7-slide deck that lands beats a 30-slide deck that crawls.
- Forgetting the appendix slide marker — exec audiences want the option to dig in, but only if they ask.
