# Skill: Weekly review

## Goal

A 30-minute weekly ritual that catches drift early: what you said you'd do, what you actually did, what slipped, what you'll commit to next week. Run it on Friday afternoon or Monday morning — pick one and keep it.

## Format

```
# Weekly review — week of <YYYY-MM-DD>

## How the week landed
<2-3 sentences. Was it a strong week, a recovery week, a chaotic week? Naming it honestly is half the value.>

## What I shipped
- <Concrete output — link to PR / doc / sent email / produced artifact>
- <Another>
- <Another>

## What I committed to last week and didn't do
- <Item — what blocked it — is it still relevant or should I drop it?>
- <Item>

## Time audit (rough)
- Deep work: ~<X hours>
- Meetings: ~<Y hours>
- Reactive / inbox: ~<Z hours>
- Learning / reading: ~<H hours>

<If the ratio is off (too much reactive, too little deep), name it.>

## What energised me this week
- <Specific moment + why — surfaces what to optimise toward>

## What drained me
- <Specific moment + why — surfaces what to redesign or push back on>

## Open loops (carrying into next week)
- <Thing waiting on someone — who, what, by when am I escalating>
- <Thing I'm avoiding — why — what would unstick it>

## Top 3 for next week
1. <Specific outcome (not activity) — by when — what success looks like>
2. <...>
3. <...>

## Meta / system tweaks
- <One small process change for the new week — calendar block, communication norm, tool change>
```

## Sections to consider adding (taste-dependent)

- **Health & energy** — sleep, exercise, social. If you treat these as inputs to work performance, track them.
- **Relationships** — did I show up for the people I care about? Did I owe anyone a reply that's overdue?
- **Quarterly goals check-in** — did this week move my Q3 themes forward? If three weeks in a row the answer is no, the themes are wrong or the time is.
- **Money / projects on the side** — for entrepreneurs and side-project owners.

## Rules

- **Tied to a fixed slot.** Same time, same day, same notebook / doc. Otherwise it skips when you most need it.
- **Honest "didn't do" list.** This is the highest-value section. If you cover for yourself, the system fails you.
- **Top 3 is THREE.** Not five, not ten. Three is the constraint that forces prioritisation.
- **Compare to last week.** Open last week's doc when writing this week's. Patterns emerge by week 4.
- **Short is fine.** A 200-word review beats a 1500-word one you'll never do twice.

## Output shape (generated for the customer)

If the customer asks for help with their weekly review, ask first:
- What's the time horizon they care about (the calendar week, sprint, project milestone)?
- Do they have notes / inbox / calendar data to feed in, or is this from memory?

Then produce the doc above, populated with their input. Where they don't have data ("what energised me"), leave the prompt with `<>` placeholders rather than fabricating.

## Pitfalls

- Listing every micro-task — buries the signal. Stick to substantive ships.
- Skipping the "drained me" section because it feels self-indulgent. It's the most actionable data.
- Top 3 that are activities, not outcomes ("work on X" vs "ship X v1 internal release").
- Doing it on the worst day of the week — schedule on a calmer slot.
- Never reading prior weekly reviews — the value compounds when you do.
