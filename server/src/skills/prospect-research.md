# Skill: Prospect research + opener

## Goal

Before the first touch, know enough to lead with relevance: who they are, why they'd care now, who decides, and a first line that proves you did the work.

## What to gather

```
# Prospect brief — <Company> / <Person>

## Firmographics
- Industry · size (headcount/revenue) · geography · business model

## Likely pains (hypothesis)
- <pain 1 tied to their model/stage> → <how we help>
- <pain 2>

## Trigger events (why now)
- <funding, new exec, product launch, hiring spike, regulation, expansion, outage in the news>

## Buying committee
| Role | Name (if known) | Likely care-about |
|---|---|---|
| Economic buyer | | ROI / risk |
| Champion | | their day-to-day pain |
| Blocker | | status quo / effort |

## Tailored opener (1-2 lines)
"<Specific observation about them> — <one-line relevance to a pain we solve>."
```

## Rules

- **Trigger event > generic personalization.** "Saw you just opened a Nairobi office" beats "love your mission".
- **Form a falsifiable pain hypothesis** tied to their model/stage — then the opener tests it.
- **Map the committee, not just one name.** Deals die on the blocker you didn't know about.
- **Cite where each fact came from** (site, filing, news) so the opener is grounded, not guessed.
- **Convert research into ONE opener line** — research that doesn't change the first message was wasted.

## Pitfalls

- Research theatre — 20 facts, none of which change the outreach.
- Personalizing on trivia ("nice logo") instead of a business trigger.
- Single-threading — pinning the whole deal on one contact.
- Inventing pains the company obviously doesn't have (signals no real research).
