# Skill: Investor update

## Goal

Investors get a clear read on the business in <5 minutes of reading. They
know what's working, what's not, what the founder is asking for, and
how the metrics moved. They can re-engage when the founder needs help
without having to ask.

## Process

1. **Pull the metrics.** Whatever the company tracks in board reports —
   typically: revenue, growth, burn, runway, churn, headcount, key
   pipeline numbers.
2. **Pull the period story.** What's the headline this month/quarter?
3. **Be honest about the lowlights.** Investors who only hear good
   news lose trust. Investors who hear honest hard news write checks.
4. **Make the asks specific.** "Intros to <X type of person>",
   "feedback on <Y>", "help with <Z hire>". Vague asks get vague help.
5. **Lead with the answer.** Top of the update: are we ahead, on-track,
   or behind?

## Output shape

```
Subject: <Company> — <Month or Quarter> update

# <Company> · <Period>

## TL;DR
<One paragraph: are we ahead / on-track / behind. What's the single most
important thing this period.>

## Highlights
- **<Win 1>** — <specific, dated, with the why-it-matters>
- **<Win 2>** — <…>
- **<Win 3>** — <…>

## Lowlights
- **<Miss / setback>** — <what happened, what we're doing about it>
- **<…>**

## Metrics

| Metric | This period | Prior | Change | Target |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Revenue / ARR | $<X> | $<X> | <+/-N%> | <…> |
| Growth rate | <N%> | <N%> | <…> | <…> |
| Burn / month | $<X> | $<X> | <…> | <…> |
| Runway | <N months> | <N months> | <…> | <…> |
| Churn (logo / $) | <%> / <%> | <…> | <…> | <…> |
| Headcount | <N> | <N> | +<N> | <plan> |
| Top-of-funnel | <N qualified opps> | <…> | <…> | <…> |

## What we're working on
- <2-3 lines on the next 30/60 days>

## Hires
- **Closed:** <Name> as <Role>, starts <date>
- **In progress:** <Role(s) we're hiring for, sourcing channel>

## Asks of you
1. **<Specific ask>** — e.g. "intro to enterprise sales leaders we
   could hire in NYC"
2. **<Specific ask>** — e.g. "feedback on the pricing experiment in
   appendix"
3. **<Specific ask>** — e.g. "warm intros to <Customer-type X>"

## Appendix (optional)
- <Charts, prior period detail, customer logos, anything supporting>

— <Founder name>
```

## Rules

- **TL;DR first.** Investors skim. Headline before details.
- **Lowlights are non-optional.** Skipping them looks evasive.
- **Asks are specific and named.** Vague asks get nothing.
- **Same metrics every period.** Don't swap out metrics that didn't
  perform — that's how trust erodes.
- **Honest variance language.** "Behind plan by X%" is better than
  "tracking against plan."

## Pitfalls

- Marketing spin. Investors see through it and trust drops.
- Hiding bad news in a long table footnote. Lead with it.
- Asking for "general support" — invites no action.
- Skipping a month when things go bad. The silence IS the message.
- Adding new metrics every time you have a good one. Discipline.
