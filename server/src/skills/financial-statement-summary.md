# Skill: Financial statement summary

## Goal

A founder or board member understands the financial position in two minutes — what the numbers are, which way they're moving, why, and what needs attention — without reading the full statements.

## Structure

```
# Financial summary — <Entity> · <period> (<vs prior period>)

## Headline
<2-3 sentences: revenue, profit/loss, cash position, and the one thing that matters most.>

## The numbers
| Metric | This period | Prior | Δ | Note |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Revenue | <x> | <x> | +X% | <driver> |
| Gross margin | <%> | <%> | … | |
| Operating profit / (loss) | <x> | <x> | … | |
| Cash & equivalents | <x> | <x> | … | runway <N> months |
| Net burn / (generation) | <x> | <x> | … | |

## What drove the change
- <revenue up because…> · <cost up because…>

## Position (balance sheet)
- Liquidity: <current ratio / cash vs near-term obligations>
- Debt: <level, covenants if any>
- Receivables / payables: <aging concern?>

## Watch list
- <the 2-3 things that need a decision or could bite next period>
```

## Key ratios (only the ones that matter)

- **Gross margin** = (revenue − COGS) / revenue. **Runway** = cash / monthly net burn. **Current ratio** = current assets / current liabilities. **DSO** = receivables / revenue × days. Compute, don't just define.

## Rules

- **Lead with the headline number and the trend**, not a definition of EBITDA.
- **Always compare** to prior period / budget — a number alone is meaningless.
- **Explain the driver**, not just the delta — "revenue +18% on two enterprise renewals" beats "revenue +18%".
- **Translate to plain language** — "we have ~9 months of cash at current burn", not "liquidity ratio 1.4".
- **State the currency and the period basis** (actual vs budget, YTD vs month).
- **Flag, don't soften.** If the trend is bad, say so plainly with the number.

## Pitfalls

- Dumping every line of the statements instead of the 5-6 numbers that matter.
- Deltas with no driver — leaves the reader to guess.
- Ratios without context (a "current ratio of 1.4" means nothing to most readers).
- Optimism spin that hides a runway or margin problem.
